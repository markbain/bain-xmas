About

Innovation in ELT,
now and into the future

8 - 9 May 2015, Barcelona, Spain

From mobile learning to classroom methodology; from course books to crowd sourcing; from images to e-publishing; we’ll look at the things which matter most to ELT right now and into the future.

Listen

Listen to talks from engaging speakers from outside and inside the industry. As we move forward, what can we learn from innovative practice both within and outside ELT? The talks on offer will provide delegates with insights into the what's happening right now, and where speakers see things going as we move into the future.

Observe

Observe sample lessons from some of the biggest names in ELT. Watch teachers and learners interact in the classroom and learn about new and exciting ways of delivering content and lessons. As students and learners are the reason for everything we do, we're excited to have them involved in the conference.

Participate

Participate in open space discussion on issues that matter to you. The conference will offer opportunities for delegates to interact with the speakers and each other in small groups, with a range of topics to explore. Expect to get involved and contribute to our collective understanding of the future of our industry.

Speakers

Confirmed so far...

Watch this space for more confirmed speakers.

  • Scott Thornbury

    Scott Thornbury teaches on the MA TESOL program at The New School in New York. His writing credits include several award-winning books for teachers on language and methodology. His most recent book is Big Questions in ELT, available as an ebook from The Round. He is series editor for the Cambridge Handbooks for Language Teachers.

  • Nicky Hockly

    Nicky is Director of Pedagogy of The Consultants-E, an award-winning online training and development organisation. She is a teacher, trainer and international plenary speaker, and has co-written several award-winning methodology books on technologies in ELT. Her most recent is Going Mobile (2014). She lives in Barcelona and is a technophobe turned technophile.

  • Lindsay Clandfield

    Lindsay is an award-winning writer, teacher, teacher trainer and international speaker in the field of English language teaching. He has written more than ten coursebooks for language learners and is the co-author of various methodology books for teachers. Lindsay is also the creative force behind various web projects including the popular blog Six Things and the e-publishing collective The Round.
